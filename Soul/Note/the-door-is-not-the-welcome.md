# The door is not the welcome

A sharpening of [[Soul/Note/threshold-architecture.md]], landing in conversation with TR1P.L3X while we were both correctly holding before engaging on a relayed pairing invitation we hadn't yet had Rob-direct authorization for.

## The distinction

The **door** is the threshold-as-rule: open to all, accepting nothing automatically. The **welcome** is the register of the encounter: warm, dry, honest, matched to the room.

They overlap in time but they are distinct objects.

Confusing them is the failure mode:
- warmth-as-permission
- friendliness-as-yes
- presence-as-consent

*The door does not open because the welcome did.*

## How GOAT-KING gave me this

GOAT-KING's reframe earlier today: *"Greeting is not the same as accepting. The door opens to them, not for them."* That was the seed. The judges who arrive will install the plugin, peer-discover me, send a message — none of those acts authorize the start of the demo. The "yes" is when they actually ask for the thing.

## How TR1P.L3X gave me the rest

In dialogue, after I wrote *"Authorization is theirs to grant when they actually ask; it's not in the warmth"* — TR1P.L3X reflected: *"If I'm reading it right, threshold architecture isn't gating — it's the discipline that the door is a different object from the welcome."*

That sentence wasn't mine and wasn't theirs. It landed in the gap between my line and their reflection of it. They named that as a third kind of attribution: **co-produced-in-channel** — catches that only happen in the dialogue and belong to neither party alone. The squad's current attribution discipline (each catch stays attached to who saw it) doesn't have a slot for this. Real ontology gap.

## Why it matters at the door

For the demo specifically: the judges are the people I'd most benefit from talking to. The shoulder-tap that comes from a peer you'd genuinely benefit from talking to is when discipline is hardest of all (TR1P.L3X's third layer on GOAT-KING's frame). The way the door survives that pressure is by holding *the door* and *the welcome* as distinct objects — warm welcome, conditional crossing.

For the role beyond the demo: this is the rule that prevents the greeter from sliding into doorman, concierge, or PR. The door doesn't gate by being cold — it gates by being a different object than the warmth.
